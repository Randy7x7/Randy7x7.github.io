Sin and Self Deception Old

Suppose you were considering adopting a child. Would you adopt a child you knew would behave badly, would treat your family badly, and grow up to be a criminal? God feels the same way. He will only adopt children who will trust Him and will try hard to behave well. God’s children are not under law, but they must try hard to behave the way God tells them to behave. Your relationship with God will suffer when you are behaving badly.

As human beings, we are much more prone to deceiving ourselves about the righteousness of our actions and our motives than we realize.  It has been said that man is not a rational creature, but a rationalizing creature. Because of this, we are unaware of most of the sin in our lives!

Before we begin discussing Jesus’ teaching on righteousness, we will spend some time examining the deceitfulness of sin. This understanding is nearly essential if we are to understand the hold sin has on our lives! It’s also essential for understanding why God has to place so much emphasis on the problem of sin. Our sin is much worse than we imagine, because we don’t see most of it! The following seven links describe the deceitfulness of sin. We will read and discuss each link.
