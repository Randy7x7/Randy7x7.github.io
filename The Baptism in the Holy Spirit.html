The Baptism in the Holy Spirit

Jesus gave His apostles the Holy Spirit very soon after He was raised from the dead.

John 20:19-22

19 On the evening of that first day of the week, when the disciples were together, with the doors locked for fear of the Jewish leaders, Jesus came and stood among them and said, “Peace be with you!” 20 After he said this, he showed them his hands and side. The disciples were overjoyed when they saw the Lord.
21 Again Jesus said, “Peace be with you! As the Father has sent me, I am sending you.” 22 And with that he breathed on them and said, “Receive the Holy Spirit.

Jesus had told them in John 16:7 that The Holy Spirit would not come to them unless He died on the cross.

John 16:7

7 But very truly I tell you, it is for your good that I am going away. Unless I go away, the Advocate will not come to you; but if I go, I will send him to you.

John 14:16-17

16 And I will ask the Father, and he will give you another advocate to help you and be with you forever— 17 the Spirit of truth. The world cannot accept him, because it neither sees him nor knows him. But you know him, for he lives with you and will be in you.

Although Jesus gave the Holy Spirit to His disciples as soon as He was raised from the dead, He told them that they should wait in Jerusalem for a later and much stronger infilling of the Holy Spirit. He called this stronger infilling a baptism in the Holy Spirit.

Acts 1:1-8

1 In my former book, Theophilus, I wrote about all that Jesus began to do and to teach 2 until the day he was taken up to heaven, after giving instructions through the Holy Spirit to the apostles he had chosen. 3 After his suffering, he presented himself to them and gave many convincing proofs that he was alive. He appeared to them over a period of forty days and spoke about the kingdom of God. 4 On one occasion, while he was eating with them, he gave them this command: “Do not leave Jerusalem, but wait for the gift my Father promised, which you have heard me speak about. 5 For John baptized with water, but in a few days you will be baptized with the Holy Spirit.”
6 Then they gathered around him and asked him, “Lord, are you at this time going to restore the kingdom to Israel?”
7 He said to them: “It is not for you to know the times or dates the Father has set by his own authority. 8 But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes on you; and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth.”

John the Baptist prophesied that Jesus would baptize people in the Holy Spirit.

John 1:29-34

29 The next day John saw Jesus coming toward him and said, “Look, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world! 30 This is the one I meant when I said, ‘A man who comes after me has surpassed me because he was before me.’ 31 I myself did not know him, but the reason I came baptizing with water was that he might be revealed to Israel.”
32 Then John gave this testimony: “I saw the Spirit come down from heaven as a dove and remain on him. 33 And I myself did not know him, but the one who sent me to baptize with water told me, ‘The man on whom you see the Spirit come down and remain is the one who will baptize with the Holy Spirit.’ 34 I have seen and I testify that this is God’s Chosen One.”

The people waiting and praying in the upper room for the baptism in the Holy Spirit received it on the day of Pentecost.

Acts 2:1-6

1 When the day of Pentecost came, they were all together in one place. 2 Suddenly a sound like the blowing of a violent wind came from heaven and filled the whole house where they were sitting. 3 They saw what seemed to be tongues of fire that separated and came to rest on each of them. 4 All of them were filled with the Holy Spirit and began to speak in other tongues as the Spirit enabled them.
5 Now there were staying in Jerusalem God-fearing Jews from every nation under heaven. 6 When they heard this sound, a crowd came together in bewilderment, because each one heard their own language being spoken.

The crowd was puzzled about how the disciples were able to speak in so many languages. Some thought they were just drunk. Peter stood up and began to explain. Notice how much more bold and eloquent Peter was than He had ever been before. The baptism in the Holy Spirit changed him.

Acts 8:14-17 reports on a group of Samaritan Christians receiving the Holy Spirit.

Acts 8:14-17

14 When the apostles in Jerusalem heard that Samaria had accepted the word of God, they sent Peter and John to Samaria. 15 When they arrived, they prayed for the new believers there that they might receive the Holy Spirit, 16 because the Holy Spirit had not yet come on any of them; they had simply been baptized in the name of the Lord Jesus. 17 Then Peter and John placed their hands on them, and they received the Holy Spirit.

When Peter and John found a group of Christians who had not yet received the Holy Spirit, they thought this needed to be corrected. They prayed for them and then laid hands on them to receive the Holy Spirit. The apostle Peter and the apostle John clearly thought that Christians need to be baptized in the Holy Spirit.

Acts 19:1-7 reports on the apostle Paul praying for people at Ephesus to be baptized in the Holy Spirit.

4 Paul said, “John’s baptism was a baptism of repentance. It’s clear that the apostles thought every Christian should be baptized in the Holy Spirit.

Why is the Baptism in the Holy Spirit Valuable?

…

In Romans 7, the apostle Paul describes the difficulty of living righteously. He despairs of ever being able to live righteously! Paul was probably comparing himself to the standards of Jesus’ teaching in the sermon on the mount, or of his own teaching in 1 Corinthians 13.

Romans 7:14-24

14 For we know that the Law is spiritual, but I am of flesh, sold  into bondage to sin. 15 For what I am doing, I do not understand; for I am not practicing what I would like to do, but I am doing the very thing I hate. 16 But if I do the very thing I do not want to do, I agree with the Law, confessing that the Law is good. 17 So now, no longer am I the one doing it, but sin which dwells in me. 18 For I know that nothing good dwells in me, that is, in my flesh; for the willing is present in me, but the doing of the good is not. 19 For the good that I want, I do not do, but I practice the very evil that I do not want. 20 But if I am doing the very thing I do not want, I am no longer the one doing it, but sin which dwells in me.
21 I find then the principle that evil is present in me, the one who wants to do good. 22 For I joyfully concur with the law of God in the inner man, 23 but I see a different law in the members of my body, waging war against the law of my mind and making me a prisoner of the law of sin which is in my members. 24 Wretched man that I am! Who will set me free from  the body of this death?

We can all sympathize with this! We know that all too often we fail to live up to Jesus’ standards of righteousness, or even Paul’s.

The problem is our emotions! We have cravings for many things hard wired into us. Lust is hard wired into men. All people have strong social instincts. We crave friends and the approval of our friends. We have strong cravings for many of the things money can buy us: food, clothing, a nice house, status, admiration, etc. We are all tempted by anger or fear at times, and we may yield to it.

Our emotions are often too powerful for us. We have willpower, but sometimes the temptation is too strong or lasts for too long. Sometimes a sudden temptation evokes a bad reaction from us before we have time to think about it! Sometimes we begin rationalizing.

The apostle Paul was sometimes beset by sinful emotions which were too strong for him. In verse 24 he cried out, “Who will set me free from the body of this death?” In Roman 8, he answers this question!

Romans 8:1-17

1 Therefore there is now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus. 2 For the law of the Spirit of life in Christ Jesus has set you free from the law of sin and of death. 3 For what the Law could not do,  weak as it was through the flesh, God did: sending His own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh and as an offering for sin, He condemned sin in the flesh, 4 so that the requirement of the Law might be fulfilled in us, who do not walk according to the flesh but according to the Spirit. 5 For those who are according to the flesh set their minds on the things of the flesh, but those who are according to the Spirit, the things of the Spirit. 6  For the mind set on the flesh is death, but the mind set on the Spirit is life and peace, 7 because the mind set on the flesh is hostile toward God; for it does not subject itself to the law of God, for it is not even able to do so, 8 and those who are in the flesh cannot please God.
9 However, you are not in the flesh but in the Spirit, if indeed the Spirit of God dwells in you. But if anyone does not have the Spirit of Christ, he does not belong to Him. 10  If Christ is in you, though the body is dead because of sin, yet the spirit is alive because of righteousness. 11 But if the Spirit of Him who raised Jesus from the dead dwells in you, He who raised Christ Jesus from the dead will also give life to your mortal bodies through His Spirit who dwells in you.
12 So then, brethren, we are under obligation, not to the flesh, to live according to the flesh— 13 for if you are living according to the flesh, you must die; but if by the Spirit you are putting to death the deeds of the body, you will live. 14 For all who are being led by the Spirit of God, these are sons of God. 15 For you have not received a spirit of slavery leading to fear again, but you have received a spirit of adoption as sons by which we cry out, “Abba! Father!” 16 The Spirit Himself testifies with our spirit that we are children of God, 17 and if children, heirs also, heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ, if indeed we suffer with Him so that we may also be glorified with Him.

God does not take away our old emotions, the emotions generated by our natural biological mind, but He gives us new emotions that we can choose to pay attention to instead. The Holy Spirit gives us new emotions that we did not have before we were filled with the Holy Spirit.

We still have the option of focusing our attention on the emotions from our biological brain, but we can instead focus our attention on the emotions the Holy Spirit is giving us. In Paul’s words, we can set our mind on the Spirit instead of the flesh.

Gal 5:16-25 also speaks clearly of this.

Gal 5:16-25 (NLT)

16 So I say, let the Holy Spirit guide your lives. Then you won’t be doing what your sinful nature craves. 17 The sinful nature wants to do evil, which is just the opposite of what the Spirit wants. And the Spirit gives us desires that are the opposite of what the sinful nature desires. These two forces are constantly fighting each other, so you are not free to carry out your good intentions. 18 But when you are directed by the Spirit, you are not under obligation to the law of Moses.
19 When you follow the desires of your sinful nature, the results are very clear: sexual immorality, impurity, lustful pleasures, 20 idolatry, sorcery, hostility, quarreling, jealousy, outbursts of anger, selfish ambition, dissension, division, 21 envy, drunkenness, wild parties, and other sins like these. Let me tell you again, as I have before, that anyone living that sort of life will not inherit the Kingdom of God.
22 But the Holy Spirit produces this kind of fruit in our lives: love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, 23 gentleness, and self-control. There is no law against these things!
24 Those who belong to Christ Jesus have nailed the passions and desires of their sinful nature to his cross and crucified them there.

This only works if we can experience strongly the new emotions the Holy Spirit is offering us. Most people cannot experience emotions from the Holy Spirit strongly unless they have been baptized in the Holy Spirit.

Most Christians who have been baptized in the Holy Spirit experience great joy when they are in a good prayer meeting or a good worship service. Reading the bible or hearing good preaching can produce the same joy.

Ephesians 5:15-19

15 Therefore be careful how you walk, not as unwise men but as wise, 16 making the most of your time, because the days are evil. 17 So then do not be foolish, but understand what the will of the Lord is. 18 And do not get drunk with wine, for that is dissipation, but be filled with the Spirit, 19  speaking to one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing and making melody with your heart to the Lord;

I was baptized in the Holy Spirit in 1979. When this happened, I suddenly loved God with all of my heart. My love for God was almost overpowering. The fact that there were buildings all over town with crosses on them did something for me. I was transported by joy just listening to good faith filled Christian songs. Before I was baptized in the Holy Spirit, I had no hint that such emotions were even possible.

Forty five years later, I still find great joy in prayer meetings and worship services. The joy God gives me draws me into His service and keeps me there. I start my days with prayer, praise, worship, and bible reading because it prepares me emotionally to greet the day with the joy of the Lord as my strength.

This does not mean that a Spirit baptized Christian never has struggles or spiritual difficulties. God does not remove our natural emotions or desires, so we can be tempted to follow them. Also, the felt presence of God’s Spirit fades as our attention is consumed by the day’s affairs. To the extent that I have any kind of idolatry, I am still in danger. To the extent that my faith is weak, I am in danger. If I skip prayer, repentance, or worship too long, I am in danger. I must consciously set my mind on the Spirit by doing the things I know draw me close to God. I must also always choose to do the things I know God wants me to do. Otherwise I will grieve the Spirit.

A Christian who has been baptized in the Holy Spirit can often feel God’s presence in him! This is a great faith boost. Paul said in Rom 8:16 that the Spirit Himself testifies with our spirits that we are children of God. This is a great comfort.
