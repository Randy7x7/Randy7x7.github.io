Idolatry

I am guilty of idolatry if I love something more than I love God. Why is this such a bad thing? Suppose I love my girlfriend more than I love having a close relationship with God. If so, I will disobey God whenever He tells me to do something that may cause me to lose my girlfriend. God is no longer my God. He is just someone who makes suggestions that I may or may not chose to obey.

Suppose I Iove status at my job more than I care what God says. Then, I will disobey God to gain status at work. Whatever I love most will control what I think and do. Loving anything more than I love God will generally lead me to disobeying God.

What were the idols that prevented people from coming to Jesus?

25 Now large crowds were going along with Him; and He turned and said to them, 26 “If anyone comes to Me, and does not hate his own father and mother and wife and children and brothers and sisters, yes, and even his own life, he cannot be My disciple. 27 Whoever does not carry his own cross and come after Me cannot be My disciple. 28 For which one of you, when he wants to build a tower, does not first sit down and calculate the cost to see if he has enough to complete it? 29 Otherwise, when he has laid a foundation and is not able to finish, all who observe it begin to ridicule him, 30 saying, ‘This man began to build and was not able to finish.’ 31 Or what king, when he sets out to meet another king in battle, will not first sit down and consider whether he is strong enough with ten thousand men to encounter the one coming against him with twenty thousand? 32 Or else, while the other is still far away, he sends a delegation and asks for terms of peace. 33 So then, none of you can be My disciple who does not give up all his own possessions.

If I want anything more than I want God, then eventually it will pull me away from Him. If I want to be a disciple of Jesus, I need to consider now what I may eventually have to give up to continue following Him. If there is anything I want more than God, then my discipleship may eventually come to an ignominious end. I should count the cost now, and make up my mind before the test comes.

Being a Christian is not a hobby. If it’s not my life, then it’s an illusion!

1 Jesus spoke to them again in parables, saying, 2 “The kingdom of heaven may be compared to a king who gave a wedding feast for his son. 3 And he sent out his slaves to call those who had been invited to the wedding feast, and they were unwilling to come. 4 Again he sent out other slaves saying, ‘Tell those who have been invited, “Behold, I have prepared my dinner; my oxen and my fattened livestock are all butchered and everything is ready; come to the wedding feast.”’ 5 But they paid no attention and went their way, one to his own farm, another to his business, 6 and the rest seized his slaves and mistreated them and killed them. 7 But the king was enraged, and he sent his armies and destroyed those murderers and set their city on fire. 8 Then he said to his slaves, ‘The wedding is ready, but those who were invited were not worthy. 9 Go therefore to the main highways, and as many as you find there, invite to the wedding feast.’ 10 Those slaves went out into the streets and gathered together all they found, both evil and good; and the wedding hall was filled with dinner guests.
11 “But when the king came in to look over the dinner guests, he saw a man there who was not dressed in wedding clothes, 12 and he said to him, ‘Friend, how did you come in here without wedding clothes?’ And the man was speechless. 13 Then the king said to the servants, ‘Bind him hand and foot, and throw him into the outer darkness; in that place there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth.’ 14 For many are  called, but few are chosen.”

People did not have to bring their own wedding clothes. The Groom supplied the wedding clothes. Wearing the wedding clothes indicated respect for the bride and groom. In this case, the wedding clothes probably represent righteousness.

Many are called but few are chosen likely means that God calls many people to become His sons and daughters, but few come. Even some of those who come do not put off their old selves and put on their new selves in Christ.

Eph 4:22-24 says,”22 . . . in reference to your former manner of life, you lay aside the old self, which is being corrupted in accordance with the lusts of deceit, 23 and that you be renewed in the spirit of your mind, 24 and put on the new self, which  in the likeness of God has been created in righteousness and holiness of the truth.”

Some Common Idols

The following is a long list of common idols. Make up a couple of stories for each that illustrate how it can be an idol. Note whenever one of them is something you personally have to fight to prevent it being an idol. All of us have such things.

Money
Job
Status
Education
Admiration
Pride
Power
Friends
Comfort
Safety
Sex
Girlfriend or Boyfriend
Family
Hobbies
Intellectual Achievements
Reading
Sports
Entertainment
Favor of (spouse, family, friends, congregation, boss, colleagues, everybody, . . .)
To Fit In with your social environment
To Avoid Conflict
To Never be Ridiculed
To be Thought Well of by All (Was Jesus thought well of by all?)
Politics
Pride in Denomination or Church

What are your most tempting idols? What do you spend a lot of time on? Entertainment, Work, House & Yard, Hobbies, Sports, Friends, Children, . . .

Do you have any potential idols that are not on this list?

For many people, idolatry is a worse spiritual problem and harder to resist than any of the other things Jesus listed in the sermon on the mount! We each need to spend time regularly asking God to help us see when we are caught up in an idolatry.

Many times when I seem to be in a dry spell with the Lord, unable to feel His presence or think and behave well, it turns out to be because I have gotten caught up in some kind of idolatry. When I discern and repent of the idolatry, the problem goes away immediately! The problem of idolatry is that pervasive and that serious, for all of us. Keep idolatry high on your list of things to watch out for and pray against. Any time you seem to be spiritually weak, check for idolatry.
